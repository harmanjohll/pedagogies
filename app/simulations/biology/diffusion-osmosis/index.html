Procedure
  1. Setup — Choose between Diffusion or Osmosis mode. Adjust the concentration using the sliders.
  2. Observe — Click "Start" to begin the simulation. Watch how particles move across the membrane.
  3. Record — Note the particle counts on each side at regular intervals. Click "Record" to log a data point.
  4. Analyse — Observe the graph. When does equilibrium occur? What affects the rate of diffusion?
Key Concepts

Diffusion is the net movement of particles from a region of higher concentration to a region of lower concentration, down the concentration gradient.

Osmosis is the net movement of water molecules from a region of higher water potential to a region of lower water potential through a partially permeable membrane.

Both processes are passive — they require no energy input from the cell.
